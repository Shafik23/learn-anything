# Vitamin D3

Evidence-based learning workspace for vitamin D3, vitamin D status, bone and calcium physiology, testing, dosing context, safety, and disease-prevention claims.

## Mission

Build enough physiology and evidence-reading skill to judge vitamin D3 claims without relying on supplement marketing or one-size-fits-all dosing advice.

Success looks like:

- Explain what vitamin D3 is, how it differs from vitamin D2, and why the body measures 25-hydroxyvitamin D.
- Separate nutrient adequacy, deficiency treatment, routine prevention, and megadose claims.
- Interpret vitamin D intake recommendations, lab thresholds, upper limits, and toxicity warnings.
- Bring informed questions to a clinician or pharmacist when personal health context, medications, pregnancy, kidney disease, calcium problems, osteoporosis, malabsorption, or lab interpretation matters.

Constraints:

- This is not personalized medical advice.
- Ground health claims in current high-trust sources.
- Treat dose, lab interpretation, and medical-condition context as clinician-guided decisions.
- Avoid tanning-bed advice, megadosing protocols, and claims that vitamin D3 prevents or treats cancer, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, infections, depression, or autoimmune disease without strong outcome evidence.

## Roadmap

- Core map: vitamin D3, D2, sunlight, food, supplements, liver activation, kidney activation, and calcium regulation.
- Evidence map: deficiency, bone health, fractures, falls, immune claims, cardiovascular claims, cancer claims, and diabetes claims.
- Safety model: RDA, UL, serum 25(OH)D, hypercalcemia, kidney stones, and medication or disease context.
- Practical interpretation: how to read supplement labels, lab reports, and guideline disagreements.
