# Vitamin D3 Sources

## Knowledge

- [NIH Office of Dietary Supplements: Vitamin D Fact Sheet for Health Professionals](https://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/VitaminD-HealthProfessional/)
  Best starting source for vitamin D physiology, forms, food sources, intake recommendations, serum 25(OH)D interpretation, risk groups, interactions, and toxicity. Use for grounding general claims.
- [Endocrine Society 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline: Vitamin D for the Prevention of Disease](https://www.endocrine.org/clinical-practice-guidelines/vitamin-d-for-prevention-of-disease)
  Current specialty-society guideline for using vitamin D to prevent disease in people without established indications for vitamin D treatment or 25(OH)D testing. Use for routine screening, empiric supplementation, age groups, pregnancy, prediabetes, and daily versus intermittent dosing context.
- [USPSTF 2021: Vitamin D Deficiency in Adults: Screening](https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/vitamin-d-deficiency-screening)
  US preventive-care recommendation for asymptomatic, community-dwelling, nonpregnant adults. Use for understanding why routine screening is controversial and why no single serum cut point defines optimal health for everyone.
- [National Academies: Dietary Reference Intakes for Calcium and Vitamin D](https://www.nationalacademies.org/read/13050)
  Primary DRI source for RDA, EAR, and UL reasoning. Use when checking the origin of US intake recommendations and upper limits.
- [NCBI Bookshelf: DRI Summary Tables](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK222881/)
  Easy-to-scan DRI tables. Use for quick checks of vitamin D recommended intakes and tolerable upper intake levels by age.
- [USPSTF 2018: Vitamin D, Calcium, or Combined Supplementation for Primary Prevention of Fractures](https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/vitamin-d-calcium-or-combined-supplementation-for-the-primary-prevention-of-fractures-in-adults-preventive-medication)
  Preventive-care recommendation focused on community-dwelling adults without known deficiency, osteoporosis, or prior fracture. Use for separating bone physiology from routine fracture-prevention claims.
- [USPSTF 2022: Vitamin, Mineral, and Multivitamin Supplementation to Prevent Cardiovascular Disease and Cancer](https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/uspstf/recommendation/vitamin-supplementation-to-prevent-cvd-and-cancer-preventive-medication)
  Use for broad chronic-disease prevention claims, especially when supplement marketing jumps from observational associations to cardiovascular or cancer prevention.
- [The BMJ 2026: Calcium, vitamin D, or combined supplementation to prevent fractures and falls](https://www.bmj.com/content/393/bmj-2025-088050)
  Recent systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized trials. Use as a current evidence update for fractures and falls in adults, while checking details such as baseline risk, setting, calcium intake, deficiency status, and osteoporosis treatment.

## Communities

- [r/ScientificNutrition](https://www.reddit.com/r/ScientificNutrition/)
  Source-required nutrition discussion. Use only for finding papers and seeing evidence critique, not for personal dosing decisions.
- Clinician or pharmacist with access to your labs, diagnoses, medication list, kidney history, calcium history, and supplement stack
  Best real-world feedback source for whether vitamin D3 supplementation, testing, or dose adjustment fits a personal medical context.
